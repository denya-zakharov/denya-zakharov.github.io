2023/ Wrike Blog

As for some parts of the website, the 2020 Brand Refresh rolled out smoothly, but some sections remained in the old design. This was also the case for Wrike Blog until 2023. The delay was mostly due to the fact that it used an older WordPress stack, requiring significant resources for a gradual update. (Left: Old blog. Right: Homepage after Brand Refresh) The difference was quite apparent, so in the beginning of 2023 it was desided to update the blog

My job was to align the design of the new website and blog. In addition, a strong focus was placed on organic traffic and the conversions it drives—mainly by incorporating CTAs and eBook promotions.

Wrike uses a 12-column grid for desktops, 6 columns for tablets, and 4 columns for mobile. The same approach was applied to the blog to ensure consistency and code reusability.

A component based system was astablished to easily manage and update the blog visuals. Some of the things were borrowed from the design system, others created for the task. New elements: Cards, Pagination, Ebook Form, Marketo Form Banner, Breadcrumbs. Adapted elements: Navigation, Footer, Buttons and their states, Typorgaphy.

Another major part of the project was the artwork by the amazing Zhanna Stgalina. The goal was to create a default cover template for each blog article category. My role was mostly technical—sharing the image sizes and requirements, while Zhanna focused on the creative direction, metaphors and creating the artwork.

This was the first iteration of the Blog Main Page. The plan was to roll out the main page first, then bulk update the article pages, author pages, and later work on the second iteration of the main page. Things that went out of scope for the first iteration are: Most Popular section, Category tabs, An updated version of search

An AI search was also planned as a part of the MPV+. A custom model would suggest topicts to read and help find needed info. Was succesfully tested at Wrike Resources page.
